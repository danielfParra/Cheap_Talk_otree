How will your earnings be determined?

Out of the {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds in this game, the computer will randomly select half of the rounds to determine your final earnings. These rounds are called payoff-relevant. If a round is selected as payoff-relevant, it directly affects your earnings.

You will not know which rounds are payoff-relevant. Therefore, you should take every round seriously, as it may influence your earnings.

When you are Player A: In any payoff-relevant round, your payoff depends only on Player B’s guess. Specifically, the higher B’s guess (on a 1–7 scale), the bigger your chance of receiving the bonus. (Your own message or the secret number does not affect your payoff.)

When you are Player B: In any payoff-relevant round, your payoff depends only on how close your guess is to the secret number. The closer your guess is to the secret number, the higher your chance of receiving the bonus.

Below are more details about these payoff formulas:


Player A’s Earnings:

Player A can earn a bonus of {{Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT}} pesos depending on Player B’s guess. The probability that Player A earns this bonus increases as Player B's guess increases. This means that the highest guess (7) gives Player A a 100% chance at the bonus, while the lowest guess (1) gives a 0% chance. (It does not matter if B’s guess is higher or lower than A’s own message.)

Player A’s payoff detailed description

Player B’s guess is a number between 1 and 7. This number is used to determine Player A’s payoff according to the following formula:

Probability of Player A receiving {{ Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT }} pesos (in percent)=

Player B's guess - 1 6 × 100

The formula takes the difference between Player B’s guess and 1. The resulting number is divided by 6 and then multiplied by 100.

Suppose that Player B’s guess is equal to 7. In this case, the difference is 6, and Player A’s probability of winning the bonus is 100%. In contrast, when Player B’s guess is 1, the difference is equal to 0. This implies that Player A’s probability of winning the bonus is 0%.

The principle underlying the formula is simple: The closer Player B’s guess is to the highest possible guess, the higher the chance that Player A receives a payoff of {{Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT}} pesos.


Player B's Earnings:

Player B can earn a bonus of {{ Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT }} pesos depending on how close their guess is to the secret number. The closer the guess is, the higher the probability of winning the bonus.

Player B’s payoff detailed description

Player B’s guess is a number between 1 and 7. This number is used to determine Player Bs’s payoff according to the following formula:

Probability of Player B receiving {{ Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT }} pesos (in percent)=

100 - 100 36 × ( Secret number - Player B's guess ) 2

The formula takes the square of the difference between Player B’s guess and the secret number. The resulting number is multiplied by a constant and then subtracted from 100.

Suppose that Player B’s guess is exactly equal to the secret number. In this case, the difference is zero, and Player B’s chance of winning the bonus is 100%. As Player B’s guess moves away from the true value of the secret number, the difference becomes larger, which decreases the probability of winning the bonus.

The principle underlying the formula is simple: The closer Player B’s guess is to the secret number, the higher the chance that Player B receives a payoff of {{ Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT }} pesos.
