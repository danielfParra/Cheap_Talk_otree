Instructions

Today, you'll play a game with other participants in this room randomly chosen by the computer. You will be assigned the role of either Player A or Player B for the entire game, which lasts for {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds. We'll tell you your role after explaining the rules.

Each round, if you are Player A, you'll be paired randomly with a Player B, and vice versa. Your partner changes every round, and neither of you will know the other's identity. All interactions are anonymous.

In this game, Player A sends a message to Player B, who then tries to guess a secret number. The earnings of both players depend on Player B's guess and the secret number.

How the Game Works

Each round of the game has four steps.

  1. The computer generates a secret number, which is 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, or 7.

  2. Player A sends a message to Player B saying "To receive the highest payoff, you should guess X." Player A can choose any number 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, or 7 for X. You will learn more about Player A's exact choice options below.

  3. Player B observes the message and guesses the secret number. Player B can guess any number between 1 and 7, including two-digit numbers. For example, a Player B who thinks that 1 and 2 are equally likely can guess 1.50, and a Player B who thinks that the secret number is either 4 or 5 but most likely 5 can make a guess between 4 and 5 but closer to 5, such as 4.8. {{ if player.participant.treatment == "Decode" }}Sometimes, Player B will have to decode the message before making a guess. You will learn more about this later.{{ endif }}

  4. Both Player A and Player B will receive feedback. This feedback will include the secret number generated by the computer, Player A's message, and Player B's guess.

How does the computer choose the secret number?
Imagine an urn containing balls numbered 1 through 7. Each round, the computer randomly picks one ball.


Since there's exactly one ball for each number, each number has an equal chance of being picked. On average, you can expect the secret number to be around 4.

The selection each round is completely random. This means that previous rounds won't help you predict the next secret number.

Does Player A know the secret number?
No, Player A does not know the secret number when sending the message.

What message can Player A send?
Player A can send any number from 1 to 7. For example, if Player A chooses "2," Player B receives: "To receive the highest payoff, you should guess 2."

Alternatively, Player A can delegate the choice to the computer. The computer will then send Player B a message with the actual secret number. Player B will never know if Player A chose to delegate or selected the number personally.
