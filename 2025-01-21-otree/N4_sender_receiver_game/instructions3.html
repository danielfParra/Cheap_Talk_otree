How does the Matching of Player As and Player Bs work?

At the start of the experiment, you'll be randomly placed into a group of {{ Constants.POOL_SIZE }} participants who share your same role (either all Player A's or all Player B's). Your group will always be matched with another group containing participants with the opposite role.

Each round, Player A’s and Player B’s from these two groups are randomly paired. This means any Player A can be matched with any Player B from the other group, and pairings will change each round.

{{ if player.participant.treatment == "Decode" }} {{ if Constants.POOL_SIZE == 5 }}

Each group of Player B’s will always include two Decode-type players and three Direct-type players. Decode-type Player B’s must decode Player A’s message, while Direct-type players do not need to decode.

Player A never knows if they're matched with a Decode-type or Direct-type Player B.

{{ elif Constants.POOL_SIZE == 4 }}

Each group of Player B’s will always include two Decode-type players and two Direct-type players. Decode-type Player B’s must decode Player A’s message, while Direct-type players do not need to decode.

Player A never knows if they're matched with a Decode-type or Direct-type Player B.

{{ endif }} {{ endif }}
