Decoding the Message

In the experiment, there will be two types of Player B, which we will call Decoder and Direct. The Decoder will have to decode the message sent by Player A. The Direct will receive the message directly.

Both types will receive the following type of message from Player A:

Player A's message:

To receive the highest payoff, you should guess X.

The Direct type will directly observe the number that Player A intended to send. The Decoder will instead see the message in the form of a mathematical expression. The decoding tasks will be of similar difficulty as the ones you solved at the beginning of the experiment. For example, if Player A intended to send the number 3, the Decoder might see the message To receive the highest payoff, you should guess ((14 − 2) × 2)/ 8).

The Decoder must answer two questions before the time runs out:

  1. What is the number that Player A intended to send?
  2. What is your guess of the secret number?
Importantly, the Decoder cannot guess the secret number until they correctly identify the number Player A tried to send.

Important:

  • Player B has {{ Constants.TIME_PER_ROUND }} seconds to make a guess. If the Decoder runs out of time, the number Player A sent is automatically submitted as the guess.
  • Decoders must wait {{ Constants.ATTEMPT_DELAY }} seconds between attempts at decoding the mathematical expression. It is not recommended to guess randomly.
