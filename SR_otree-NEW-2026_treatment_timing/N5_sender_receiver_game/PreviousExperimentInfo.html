Information from a Previous Experiment

Context

During today's experiment, you will observe information that was generated by participants who took part in a previous session of this same study.

In that previous session, participants were assigned the role of Player A or Player B. There is a secret number between 1 and 7. Player A's task was to send a message to Player B, not knowing the secret number. Player A could either send any number between 1 and 7, or delegate the decision to the computer, in which case the message contained the true secret number.

After receiving the message, Player B guessed the secret number.

Your Task Today

Today, your task is closely related to the task faced by Player B in the earlier session. You will read the actual messages that participants in the role of Player A sent in the past. After seeing a message, you will have to guess the secret number.

About the Instructions Below

To help you understand the decisions that the earlier participants made, we will now show you the same instructions that they received. All messages you will observe today were generated under the exact rules described in those instructions.

Because your task shares many features with that of the Player B participants from the earlier session, it is important that you read these instructions carefully.
