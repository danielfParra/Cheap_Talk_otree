Instructions

Today, you will be randomly paired with another person to play a game. he computer will assign you a random role: Player A or Player B. You will keep this role for {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds. On the next screen, we will tell you if you are Player A or Player B. In each round, Player A will be paired with a new Player B, and vice versa. Your partner will change in every round. Neither of you will know the identity of the other, and all interactions will be anonymized by the computers.


Game overview


The game has four steps in each round.

  1. The computer picks a secret number. It can be 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6.

  2. Player A sends a message to Player B that says, "The secret number is X." Player A can choose any number 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6 for X. For example, if Player A chooses “2”, Player B will receive the message "The secret number is 2." Player A does not know the actual secret number when sending the message.

  3. Player B sees the message and guesses the secret number. Player B can guess any number from 1 to 6, including two-digit numbers. For example, a Player B who thinks that 1 and 2 are equally likely can guess 1.50, and a Player B who thinks that the secret number is either 4 or 5 but most likely 5 can make a guess between 4 and 5 but closer to 5, such as 4.8.

  4. Both Player A and Player B will receive feedback. This feedback shows the secret number, the message sent, and Player B’s guess.

How does the computer pick the secret number?
Think of the computer as a machine that picks one ball from a jar. The jar has six balls, numbered 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6.


Each ball has the same chance of being picked, so each number from 1 to 6 is equally likely. Consequently, in the absence of any information, you can reasonably assume that the secret number will, on average, be equal to 3.5.

The number chosen in each round is completely random. This means that what happened in earlier rounds doesn’t affect the next round. Don’t try to guess based on past rounds; the results are always new.
