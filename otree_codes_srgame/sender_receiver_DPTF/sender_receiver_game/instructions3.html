How does the matching of Player As and Player Bs work?

At the beginning of the experiment, you will be randomly placed into groups of ten participants: five Player As and five Player Bs. In every round of the experiment, Player As and Player Bs are randomly re-matched into five pairs. This means that any Player A in your group can be matched with any Player B in your group during a round.

The computer does the matching for every round. It randomly selects a Player A and a Player B to create each pair. Watch the video below for an illustration of how the matching works.
