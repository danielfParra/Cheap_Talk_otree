How your task differs from the previous experiment
What you just read vs. what you will do

The instructions you just read described how participants in the previous experiment played. In that session, Player B directly guessed the secret number by choosing a number between 1 and 7.

Your task is different. Instead of entering a direct guess, you will report your belief about the likelihood that Player A delegated to the computer (in which case the sent number equals the true number). You will move a slider from 0% (Player A surely did not delegate) to 100% (Player A surely delegated).

How the computer uses your belief

Once you report your belief, the computer will automatically calculate and submit the guess that best maximises your chances of earning the bonus given what you stated.

This means your best strategy is to report your true belief — the more accurately your belief reflects Player A's actual behaviour, the higher your expected earnings.

The next pages will ask some comprehension questions and then show you a short tutorial of the belief slider before the game begins.

How the computer calculates the optimal guess

Let p be your stated belief (as a fraction between 0 and 1) about the likelihood that Player A delegated to the computer (was honest), and let m be the message you observe. The computer submits:

Optimal guess = p × m + (1 − p) × 4

This formula maximizes your expected earnings. When Player A is honest (probability p), the message m equals the secret number, so the best guess is m. When Player A is not honest (probability 1 − p), the message carries no information and the best guess is the unconditional mean of the secret number, which is 4. The optimal guess is a weighted average of these two cases.

How today's game works

The instructions you just read described a previous experiment in which Player B's payoff depended on one hidden secret number.

Your task is different. In each round, the message you see comes from a bundle of 4 previous senders who all sent that same message. Some of those senders delegated to the computer, and some chose the message strategically. You will be told how many delegated.

How your guess is evaluated

You still submit one guess between 1 and 7.

That single guess is then compared with the 4 secret numbers in the bundle, and your round performance is based on your average accuracy across those 4 comparisons.
